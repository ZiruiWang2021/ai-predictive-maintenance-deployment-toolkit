# Bearing Fault Guide

Synthetic demo document for portfolio use only. It is not a real OEM manual.

## Purpose

This guide summarises common bearing fault indicators for predictive maintenance demonstrations. It supports diagnosis when vibration, bearing temperature, noise, lubrication history, and RUL estimates indicate possible deterioration.

## Symptoms

Bearing issues often appear as increasing vibration, elevated bearing temperature, abnormal noise, unstable current draw, or a repeated alarm after lubrication or alignment work. A single abnormal reading should be validated before escalation. A persistent trend across vibration and temperature is more concerning than one isolated spike.

## Diagnostic checks

Inspect vibration trend, bearing temperature, lubrication quality, contamination, recent maintenance activity, alignment, shaft loading, and mounting condition. Check whether vibration increased immediately after lubrication, because over-lubrication or incorrect lubricant can also create heat and abnormal vibration.

## Recommended action

If vibration is elevated but temperature is normal, inspect and monitor more frequently. If vibration and temperature rise together, plan an intervention in the next safe maintenance window. If predicted RUL is also low, classify the case as high risk and avoid deferring inspection without engineering approval.

## Limitations
