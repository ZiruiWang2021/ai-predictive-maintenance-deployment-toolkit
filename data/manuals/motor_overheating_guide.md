# Motor Overheating Guide

Synthetic demo document for portfolio use only. It is not a real OEM manual.

## Purpose

This guide describes common checks when a motor temperature trend rises above normal operating range. It is designed for a predictive maintenance demo where sensor readings, fault descriptions, and retrieved manual evidence are combined into a maintenance recommendation.

## Symptoms

Typical symptoms include rising motor temperature, elevated current, reduced cooling airflow, smell of overheating insulation, frequent thermal alarms, or temperature increasing after load changes. When high temperature appears together with vibration, the issue may involve bearing wear, shaft misalignment, lubrication breakdown, or mechanical friction.

## Checks

First validate the temperature reading. Confirm sensor calibration, mounting, wiring, and whether a redundant thermometer or secondary sensor agrees with the reading. Then inspect cooling airflow, fan condition, blocked ventilation, dust on cooling fins, ambient temperature, recent load changes, lubrication condition, and current imbalance across phases.

## Recommended action

If temperature is elevated but stable, inspect and monitor the trend more frequently. If temperature keeps rising, reduce load if safe and schedule maintenance. If temperature is high with vibration or current imbalance, classify the case as higher risk and plan inspection in the next safe maintenance window.

## Limitations

This is synthetic demo data. Real maintenance decisions must use OEM manuals, site procedures, safety rules, and qualified engineering judgement.
