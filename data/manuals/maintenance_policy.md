# Maintenance Decision Policy

Synthetic demo document for portfolio use only. It is not a real company policy.

## Purpose

This policy note defines how predictive maintenance outputs should support maintenance planning. The goal is decision support, not autonomous scheduling.

## Risk levels

High risk means the asset may require inspection or intervention in the next safe maintenance window. Medium risk means the asset should be inspected, signals validated, and monitored more frequently. Low risk means normal monitoring can continue. Unknown risk means more evidence is required before making a maintenance decision.

## Human approval

High-risk recommendations should be reviewed by qualified maintenance or asset engineering staff before action. Human approval is especially important for route-critical assets, service-disrupting interventions, stale data, uncertain evidence, or recommendations that conflict with operator experience.

## Evidence requirements

A maintenance recommendation should include the task interpretation, tools used, retrieved evidence, source documents, RUL estimate when available, risk level, next action, and limitations. If evidence is weak or outside the knowledge base, the system should explicitly say so.

## Limitations

This policy is synthetic demo data. Real policies must be approved by safety, compliance, asset engineering, and operations teams.
